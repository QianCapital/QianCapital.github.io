Software Engineering

Site Reliability Engineers (SREs) manage and support the firm’s internal infrastructure and monitor trading systems’ health and status. Often, trading systems in production will have disasters and may break or have high latency. Engineers are responsible for debugging and fixing these issues and making sure all trading systems are up and running normally and efficiently. Engineers will work to continuously improve and enhance the design and performance of the firm’s trading infrastructure.

As such, they work closely with developers to understand how the underlying systems and backends work, and they closely with traders to understand and diagnose and resolve system issues.

There are also other engineers including network engineers and FPGA engineers that focus on the performance, efficiency, and latency/speed of the trading systems and platforms. They focus on building and improving the trading firm’s infrastructure to support and enhance lower latency and more efficient deployment of trading strategies. They monitor the firm’s and trading system’s network health and connection to brokers, clients, exchanges, etc. They also work to debug any issues with data gaps, high latency, etc.

Engineers are like Air Traffic Control that monitors that monitors the airplane systems during flight and ensures everything is working properly and that all flight systems are healthy and in check. They maintain the health of the flight systems and assist pilots with any system malfunctions and will work to fix those. In the event that issues occur, they communicate with the pilots to understand the issues and direct and guide them to resolving those issues. Otherwise, if there are no issues, their job is to monitor the flight path of the planes and status of communication systems to ensure nothing unusual happens. They monitor the systems’ health and work to ensure all systems are up and working as intended.
