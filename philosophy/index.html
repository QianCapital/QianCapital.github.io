Our Philosophy

"God does not place dice with the markets."

On Markets

Albert Einstein was a critic of quantum mechanics although he made significant contributions in the development of the theory. The Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle states we cannot know or predict the speed and position of a particle with perfect precision, so everything is probabilistic (i.e. rolling a die). However, Einstein rejected this principle and believes that although equations in quantum mechanics appear probabilistic, the true laws and equations governing the physics of the universe is deterministic and that our universe is not completely random and chaotic with no hope of modeling exactly how the universe operates.

This principle can also be applied to financial markets. We reject the commonly accepted “Random Walk Hypothesis” about the financial markets and believes in the power of building systems using data to model the markets and drive trading decisions.
