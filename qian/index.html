The QIAN Process


At Qian Capital, our investment philosophy is grounded in a deeply thoughtful, epistemological, and iterative process we call QIAN — an acronym that reflects the way we approach markets, data, learning, and decision-making:

Q

Question

Origin of inquiry. Curiosity. The desire to understand.
We begin with curiosity. Every signal, anomaly, or inefficiency starts with a question — a deliberate challenge to consensus or assumptions. Questions drive both our understanding of the world and the markets. They are the foundation of our research and the catalyst for our investment decisions. Our culture is to challenge commonly accepted principles as well as explore unchartered territories. We believe that the best ideas often come from unexpected places, and we encourage our team to think outside the box.

I

Intuition

Pre-analytical gut sense, pattern recognition, market instinct.
Before diving into data, we formulate an initial hypothesis from the tacit knowledge learned through experience, observation, and deep pattern recognition of the markets, and life in general. We believe intuition is an essential part of learning. Intuition-driven research is purposeful and either confirms our perspective, or improves them. Intuition is only the starting point of our process. It is the first step in a rigorous analytical journey that will either validate or challenge our initial assumptions.

A

Analysis

Breakdown of statistical analysis, modeling, and testing.
Using rigourous quantitative analysis, statistical reasoning and modeling, machine learning/AI, we validate the efficacy of our intuition and come to a conclusion for the initial question. In some cases, the conclusion is too high complexity to be human-understandable, but can be understood by AI. We accept the limits and extent of human reasoning capabilities, and embrace AI as a companion to help us understand the world.

N

Notion

Emergent concept, ready to be acted on or further refined.
From this synthesis emerges a notion — a well-formed, actionable concept. Notions are constantly changing and being refined with new knowledge and data. These notions eventually form our nexus and drive our strategies and evolve over time as markets shift. The QIAN process then repeats to either update and refine our existing notions, or formualte new ones.
